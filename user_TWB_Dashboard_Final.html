Community Profile Insights

Our community is remarkably well-educated: 80% have university degrees, with 34% holding Master's degrees or higher. This expertise is invaluable for quality humanitarian translation.

Geographically, Nigeria leads with 192 respondents (15%), followed by Italy, Argentina, and France. The African continent is strongly represented, showing TWB's important work in the region.

Most of you work in Communications & Language Services (28%), followed by Education (14%) and Health & Medicine (8%). This professional background directly supports TWB's mission.

Experience Insights: What This Tells Us

Transcription (630 interested) is your most desired new role, followed by representing your language community (571) and becoming a language expert (531). This shows a strong desire to expand beyond traditional translation.

Nearly half of you (46%) have been with TWB for 3+ years, showing remarkable loyalty. Yet 31% haven't been able to contribute yet, largely due to the language pair availability issue identified earlier.

The data suggests TWB should prioritize: 1) Expanding language pair coverage, 2) Creating more transcription opportunities, and 3) Developing language community leadership roles.

Satisfaction Deep Dive

Your Net Promoter Score of +32 is considered "Great" in industry standards (above +20). This means you're genuinely enthusiastic about TWB and actively recommend it to others.

However, the 19% who are dissatisfied deserve attention. Cross-referencing with other data suggests their main pain points are: 1) Not finding projects in their language, 2) Platform usability issues, and 3) Wanting more human connection with the community.

The good news? These are all solvable challenges. Your feedback is being heard and will shape TWB's priorities for 2025.

AI Insights: What Your Concerns Show

Your #1 concern (67%) is that AI might lower translation quality in underserved languages. This is exactly the type of languages TWB focuses on. Your concern shows deep understanding of the issue.

The concern about misinformation (58%) shows awareness that biased AI training data could spread harmful content. And 55% worry about reduced opportunities for human translators.

What's clear: you see AI as a tool to enhance your work, not replace your judgment. The data shows strong interest in contributing to language tech (transcription, voice recording) with proper training. 89% want training before diving in.

4BC Deep Dive: Your Path Forward

What you want to do: Translation for underrepresented languages (697), text/voice data initiatives (655), and mapping underserved languages (551). These match perfectly with 4BC's mission.

What you need: When asked what support you require, 1,121 of you (89%!) said training through courses or webinars. You're ready to learn and contribute; just show you how!

How you want to connect: 38% prefer virtual-only, 33% want both virtual and in-person, and only 7% want in-person only. This suggests hybrid community-building will reach the most volunteers.

The opportunity: With 75% unaware of 4BC but 78% eager to connect, there's enormous potential for growth. The community is waiting to be activated.
